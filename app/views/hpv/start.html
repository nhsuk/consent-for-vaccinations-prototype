The HPV vaccine helps protect against cancers caused by the human papillomavirus (HPV), including:

  • cervical cancer
  • some mouth and throat cancers
  • some cancers of the anal and genital areas

It also helps protect against genital warts.

The HPV vaccine is given as 2 injections into the upper arm spaced at least 6 months apart.

The 1st dose of the vaccine is offered to girls and boys aged 12 and 13 in school Year 8. The 2nd dose is offered 6 to 24 months after the 1st dose.
